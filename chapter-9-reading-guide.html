Chapter 9 Reading Guide

Classical Paradigm heavily religious: basically says that ‘man is special and favoured in his reverence of God’ █ Humans are at the centre of the Universe. █ Humans were placed there by God(s). █ Human purpose is the worship of God(s). █ Religion is the path to truth. Influence of Copernicus caused a paradigm shift from the classical paradigm to the enlightenment paradigm by proving that the solar system orbits around the Sun established that rather than Earth (man) being at the centre of the Universe, it was the Sun felt insignificant, changed our perception of the human condition Since humans were no longer at the center of the world, they turned to what made them different from the rest of the living world: their ability to reason Using minds, we can understand and control, bring order to chaos and design to order Humankind now has to earn its status on ‘celestial pyramid’ Ultimately a way for humans to assert their sense of superiority Enlightenment Paradigm more scientific: basically says that ‘man is special in his ability to reason █ Man is rationally superior to all things. █ Reason is a gift of God. █ Man’s purpose is to investigate the Universe. █ Science is the source of truth. Modern Paradigm postmodernism: basically says that ‘there is no meaning to anything, as nothing is predetermined’ █ Humans are random evolutionary accidents. █ Humankind is one of a billion biological species. █ There is no God. █ There is no purpose to life. █ There is no path to truth. List some problems of the notion of alien life for monotheistic religions It counters their belief that humans are the center of the universe, or that humans are superior to all things. It goes against the paradigm that humans have learnt. Define Culture (p.185) The social, personal, intellectual, spiritual and commercial environment of a community. It dictates the values that a society holds, and subsequently affects many of the standards that a society holds. It is how humans interpret the world. Examples include: Handshakes when you greet somebody Fighting for the bill (Chinese) Saying Sorry Holding the door Tipping The example of Japanese language. What is its function? The Japanese value politeness and order above all else, and this permeates into language. Associates an individual with a relative social position, taking into account gender, age and importance in a company. It is expected to show signs of respect in language at all times to not stand out in Japanese society. Humility and modesty are pillars of Japanese culture, often selling yourself short and raising others above you, usually as a sign of respect. As such, there is often a deep disconnect between individuals or groups of different status. A specific example is one of Japanese parents, where no matter how well you know their child, it will always remain incredibly difficult to get to know them, because of honorifics and how odd it is to use honorifics when asking personal questions. In contrast, Western culture does not employ honorifics and always addresses another individual as ‘you’, no matter social status, allowing for closer personal connection between individuals from the get-go Value group harmony over self-expression basically, language can enforce culture Summarise the role of paradigms for humans We have little or no conscious choice over the paradigms of our society Play a large role in the way we interpret information and allows us to realize that everything is simply interpretation There are basic paradigms and grand paradigms (of which we may not be aware) Ex. of basic paradigm: “My name is Nick” Ex. of grand paradigm: “I’m deeply misunderstood” Many paradigms (including the examples above) refer to our place in the Universe and how we fit into the bigger picture Paradigms are basically assumptions about humans that build up the image of what it is to be a human in the Universe (Humans are smarter than cockroaches, God loves me, there is no God, humans are controlled by fate, etc) When someone/something threatens our paradigms, we feel lost and disturbed because humans usually like to stick to things that they know, rather than to move on to new things The model/assumption about what a human is has evolved over time...
